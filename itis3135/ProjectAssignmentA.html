  5. Page Design
    1. Introductory Page
      1. Purpose: Serve as an about, generalizing the important smaller information found throughout the rest of the site onto a singular spot, relevant to employers just skimming the site
      2. Audience: All Intended Users
      3. Content: Information about the client, a blurb about history, as well as links to further information
      4. Not the page for user data entry
      5. Page includes links to other pages, serving as an entryway, when links are clicked they are transferred to other pages.
    2. Upload Page
      1. Purpose: Provide functionality to users by having a site accessible to only them that allows for them to upload their information to the rest of the site
      2. Audience: The client only
      3. Content: Vector for information upload, has a form that allows for document upload, as well as links to the rest of the site
      4. A page for client data entry
      5. Page includes upload forms that allow for document entry or modular changes to the Documents or Links pages
    3. Documents/Accomplishments Page
      1. Purpose: Showcasing of all documents and accomplishments in one area
      2. Audience: All Intended Users
      3. Content: Primarily documents, as well as tabs to switch between these documents. Links to the other websites as well.
      4. No user entry
      5. Page includes subfiles that showcase pdfs or relevant do files in the browser
    4. Past Employments/Links Page
      1. Purpose: shows past employers, as well as links, including to their websites. Doubles as a showcase of what tasks the client performed at the place of employment.
      2. Audience: All Intended Users
      3. Content: Hyperlinks and text talking about past experiences.
      4. No user entry
      5. Tabs switching between places of employment or possibly a timeline showing time progression
    5. Contact Page
      1. Purpose: Is the central page for employers to contact the client. Includes contact information about the client.
      2. Audience: Primarily future employers and scouting agencies
      3. Content: Listing of contact information, and forms for users to enter in their contact info, or potentially send an email straight from in website
      4. User Entry
  6. Dynamic Functionality
    1. Tab System that allows users to easily switch between relevant information, utilizing forms and buttons on the main page. Utilized on Past Employments and Documents pages.
